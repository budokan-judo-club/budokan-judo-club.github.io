Budokan Judo

Learn Judo at one of New Zealand's oldest Judo clubs

Judo

Judo is a modern martial art that evolved from the Samurai's unarmed combat style, known as Jujitsu. Competitions in Jujitsu had to be highly structured to prevent combatants being killed or maimed! Jigoro Kano (1860 - 1938 ) is credited as being one to the first to take Jujitsu, remove excessively dangerous elements and turn it into the modern Olympic sport of Judo.

Instructor

Chief Instructor, Alan Hoverd, holds the title of 5th Dan (Degree) black belt. He has represented New Zealand on several occasions; most recently in 2009 at the Kodokan in Japan - the spiritual 'home' of Judo. Alan has been practicing Judo for 47 years.

Club

The Budokan Judo Club in Wellington is one of New Zealand's oldest, and boasts a great depth of instruction. There are 8 black belt instructors in the club. The youngest of whom has been practicing Judo for 27 years.

Atmosphere

The club encourages a family atmosphere with siblings and parents being urged to join in on the mat and take advantage of the fitness, self defence, discipline and sport to be gain from Judo.
